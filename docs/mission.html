Our Mission

Faith in action — supporting the poorest Coptic families in Egypt for over 30 years.

What We Believe

The Coptic Aid Foundation was established on a simple conviction: that faith without action is empty. Inspired by the words of James 1:27 and Matthew 25, we believe caring for the poor is not optional — it is a calling.

We support needy Coptic Christian families, orphans, widows, and university students across Egypt. We send monthly financial aid, fund small business projects, and mobilize emergency relief when crisis strikes.

We do not have a political mandate. Our goal is to help vulnerable families survive with dignity while sustaining their faith.

Our Story

[PHOTO: Founding members or early work — archival if available]

The Coptic Aid Foundation was born from a simple invitation. Mr. Philip Girgis was asked by the late Father Rofael Nakhla to address the faithful at a local church. That moment planted a seed.

In Egypt, the early work was shaped by priests and community workers deeply embedded with the poor. A key moment came when His Grace Bishop Paula of Gharbia granted permission for Father Mikhail Sobhi to serve as the Foundation's official representative in Egypt — a role that gave our work roots on the ground.

In Canada, the Foundation operates under the auspices of the Diocese of Montreal, with the blessing of His Grace Bishop Boulous.

In 1992, Mr. Girgis was honoured to meet three times with His Holiness Pope Shenouda III, who — after examining the Foundation's work thoroughly — gave his blessing in writing, in both Arabic and English. That blessing has guided us ever since.

The Foundation has been operating from Montreal, Quebec, Canada since its founding, with a fully volunteer board and zero administrative overhead.

Our work is endorsed by the Coptic Orthodox Church at both the local and universal level:

Montreal Diocese

The Coptic Aid Foundation operates under the auspices of the Diocese of Montreal. We are honoured to have the blessing and endorsement of His Grace Bishop Boulous, Bishop of Montreal, who supports our humanitarian mission.

Universal Church

  • His Holiness Pope Tawadros II — current blessing and support
  • His Holiness Pope Shenouda III — founding blessing (1992), renewed multiple times
  • 10 bishops and 30 priests across Egypt have supported our work through the years
  • Our official representative in Egypt: Rev. Fr. Mikhail Sobhi, St. Mikhael Coptic Orthodox Church, Koufour, Tanta

This church network — from Montreal to Egypt — is how we identify families, verify needs, and ensure aid reaches the right people.

Who We Serve

[PHOTO: Upper Egypt landscape or community — dignified, not poverty-exploiting]

Egypt is home to approximately 10 million Coptic Christians. At least 20% of the Coptic community lives in poverty — most in Upper Egypt as subsistence farmers, and thousands in urban areas like Cairo's Zabbaleen neighbourhood, living on the margins.

We serve:

  • Needy families unable to meet basic needs
  • Widows without income support
  • Orphaned children
  • University students from poor families
  • Families who can work but lack the tools or capital to start

All recipients are identified and vetted by clergy of the Coptic Orthodox Church in Egypt. Aid is sent monthly without interruption — even if a donor is late or stops giving.
