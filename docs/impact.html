Program 1 — Monthly Financial Aid

[PHOTO: Family receiving aid, or dignified portrait of recipient family. To be provided.]

Our core program provides a regular monthly payment to needy families, widows, and orphans identified and vetted by clergy of the Coptic Orthodox Church in Egypt.

How it works

  • Families are selected by local priests who know their circumstances directly
  • Aid is disbursed at the beginning of each month without interruption
  • Families continue to receive support even if a donor is temporarily unable to give
  • Field workers visit recipients annually and send updated reports

Who is supported

  • Needy families unable to cover basic living expenses
  • Widows without income
  • Orphaned children
  • University students from poor families

Program 4 — Small Business Projects

[PHOTO: Person working at small trade — sewing, bakery, market stall. To be provided.]

We believe the most dignified path out of poverty is self-sufficiency. For families who are willing and able to work, we fund small business projects that provide the tools or capital they need to earn their own income.

How it works

  • Projects typically cost as little as $200 CAD (approximately 500 Egyptian Pounds at founding rates)
  • Examples include: a sewing machine, a small cart, tools for a trade, seed capital for a micro-business
  • Families are selected based on capacity and willingness to work
  • The goal is independence — families graduate out of the monthly aid program when able

Program 3 — Emergency Relief

[PHOTO: Community support or relief context. To be provided.]

When crisis strikes — whether natural disaster, sudden illness, displacement, or sectarian violence — we mobilize quickly to provide emergency funds and essentials to the families who need it most.

What emergency relief covers

  • Emergency cash transfers to families in acute need
  • Medicine and basic healthcare support
  • Shelter assistance for the homeless
  • Clothing and essential items
  • Support for new immigrants adjusting to a new country

Program 2 — Education Support

[PHOTO: Students or school context in Egypt. To be provided.]

We support students from poor families who would otherwise be unable to continue their education. We currently support 728 university students — education is the most lasting investment in a family's future.

  • University students from low-income Coptic families
  • Support for children in primary and secondary school where needed
  • Recipients selected by local clergy based on need and academic potential

Program 5 — Medical Support

[PHOTO: Medical care or healthcare context in Egypt. To be provided.]

Access to healthcare is out of reach for many poor Coptic families in Egypt. Universal medical care is non-existent, leaving the most vulnerable with no support when illness strikes. We help bridge that gap.

What medical support covers

  • Medicine and basic healthcare essentials for families in need
  • Support for families facing acute or chronic illness without means to pay
  • Coordination through local clergy who identify urgent medical needs
