Program 1 — Monthly Financial Aid

[PHOTO: Family receiving aid, or dignified portrait of recipient family. To be provided.]

Our core program provides a regular monthly payment to needy families, widows, and orphans identified and vetted by clergy of the Coptic Orthodox Church in Egypt.

How it works

  • Families are selected by local priests who know their circumstances directly
  • Aid is disbursed at the beginning of each month without interruption
  • Families continue to receive support even if a donor is temporarily unable to give
  • Field workers visit recipients annually and send updated reports
  • Two annual feast grants — Christmas and Easter — in addition to regular monthly support

Who is supported

  • Needy families unable to cover basic living expenses
  • Widows without income
  • Orphaned children

Program 2 — Education Support

[PHOTO: University students in Egypt. To be provided.]

Education is the most lasting investment in a family's future. Today, 728+ university students are actively receiving monthly support from the Coptic Aid Foundation. Over 20 years, thousands of students have graduated with the Foundation's help — many going on to find employment and lift their entire families out of poverty.

  • University students from low-income Coptic families
  • Monthly stipend equal to the family support amount
  • Recipients selected by local clergy based on need and academic potential
  • Support continues through graduation — stopped upon completion or dropout

Program 3 — Emergency Relief

[PHOTO: Community support or relief context. To be provided.]

When crisis strikes — whether natural disaster, sudden illness, displacement, or sectarian violence — we mobilize quickly to provide emergency funds and essentials to the families who need it most.

What emergency relief covers

  • Emergency cash transfers to families in acute need
  • Clothing and essential items
  • Shelter assistance for the homeless

Program 4 — Medical Support

[PHOTO: Medical care or healthcare context in Egypt. To be provided.]

Access to healthcare is out of reach for many poor Coptic families in Egypt. Universal medical care is non-existent, leaving the most vulnerable with no support when illness strikes. We help bridge that gap — supporting dozens of families annually with serious and chronic illness costs that are beyond the means of the family or their local church.

What medical support covers

  • Medicine and basic healthcare essentials for families in need
  • Support for families facing acute or chronic illness without means to pay
  • Coordination through local clergy who identify urgent medical needs

Program 5 — Small Business Projects

[PHOTO: Person working at small trade — sewing, bakery, market stall, livestock. To be provided.]

We believe the most dignified path out of poverty is self-sufficiency. For families who are willing and able to work, we fund small business projects that provide the tools or capital they need to earn their own income.

How it works

  • Projects typically cost between $200–$500 CAD
  • Examples include: sewing machines, workshop tools, grocery supplies, livestock — whatever gives a family the means to earn their own income
  • Families are selected based on capacity and willingness to work
  • The goal is independence — families graduate out of the monthly aid program when able

Program 6 — Community Support

[PHOTO: Family in their home, or community gathering. To be provided.]

Over the past decade, the Foundation has expanded its support to address life's most fundamental needs — helping families through pivotal moments that no family should face alone.

Marriage support

For approximately the last 10 years, we have helped poor families furnish a basic home for their daughters starting a new life. A simple home — a bed, basic appliances, essential furnishings — can mean everything to a young couple beginning their life together in Christ.

Housing needs

We support families living in homes without a roof, without sanitation, without a door, or without a bed to sleep on. Every year, dozens of families receive help to meet these most basic human needs.

Coverage in Egypt

Our work began in Tanta, Gharbia in the 1980s — with 10 to 20 widows and orphan mothers. Today our network spans more than 48 regions across Egypt, from Alexandria in the north to Aswan in the south. Aid reaches families in rural Upper Egypt, urban Cairo, the Nile Delta, and everywhere in between.
