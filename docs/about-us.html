Our Story

Our Motto — from the Divine Liturgy

Aid for the Poor

Aide aux Pauvres

معونة للمساكين

[PHOTO: Founding members or early work in Tanta — archival if available]

The story of the Coptic Aid Foundation begins not in Montreal, but in Tanta, Gharbia — in the heart of Egypt's Delta region. In the 1980s, a group of blessed servants were already quietly caring for widows, orphans, and the poorest among their community. They collected small amounts — oil, sugar, rice, tea — and more importantly, they invited those who could not afford to attend church to come, to receive communion, and to know they were not forgotten.

Our late founder, Philip Girgis, lived in Montreal and knew these servants in Tanta. Moved by what they were doing, he began collecting donations from the Coptic community in Montreal to support their work. He was guided always by the words of James 1:27 — the scripture that became the Foundation's cornerstone. He was certain the work was not his own; he believed it was God who moved the hearts of people to give.

What began with 10 to 20 widows and orphan mothers in Tanta grew slowly — to hundreds, then thousands of families across all of Egypt. In 1989, the Coptic Aid Foundation was formally registered in Canada, taking its name and motto — "Aid for the Poor" — directly from the Divine Liturgy prayer that every Coptic Christian knows by heart.

Today, the Foundation supports over 7,150 families across more than 48 regions of Egypt, from Alexandria to Aswan — all through the generosity of donors and the dedication of volunteers who receive no salary or compensation.

In Canada, the Foundation operates under the auspices of the Diocese of Montreal, with the blessing and endorsement of His Grace Bishop Boulous.

In 1992, Philip Girgis was honoured to meet three times with His Holiness Pope Shenouda III, who — after thoroughly examining the Foundation's work — gave his blessing in writing, in both Arabic and English. That blessing has guided us ever since.

"Bless the fullness of the year out of your goodness, for the sake of the poor of your people, for the widow, the orphan, the stranger, and the visitor, and for us all who hope in you and seek your Holy Name." — Divine Liturgy, Prayer for the Crops
"Pure and genuine religion in the sight of God the Father means caring for orphans and widows in their distress." — James 1:27 — The founding scripture of the Coptic Aid Foundation

Who We Serve

[PHOTO: Upper Egypt landscape or community — dignified, not poverty-exploiting]

Egypt is home to approximately 10–15 million Coptic Christians. At least 20% of the Coptic community lives in poverty — most in Upper Egypt as subsistence farmers, and thousands in urban areas like Cairo's Zabbaleen neighbourhood, living on the margins. The Coptic Aid Foundation is one of the largest external sources of direct aid to poor Coptic families in Egypt.

We serve:

  • Needy families unable to meet basic needs
  • Widows without income support
  • Orphaned children
  • University students from poor families — 728+ currently receiving monthly support, and thousands of graduates over the decades who have gone on to find employment and lift their families out of poverty
  • Families who can work but lack the tools or capital to start a small business
  • Young women from poor families preparing to start a new home — supported through the marriage process for approximately the last 10 years
  • Families lacking basic housing necessities — shelter, sanitation, furnishings

All recipients are identified and vetted by clergy of the Coptic Orthodox Church in Egypt. Aid is sent monthly without interruption — even if a donor is late or stops giving.

How We Operate

  • Financial aid is disbursed at the beginning of each month without interruption — even if a donor is late or stops giving
  • Field workers in Egypt visit every recipient annually and submit updated reports
  • The Foundation's accounts are verified annually by a reputable Chartered Accountant
  • No officer, director, or member may borrow from the Foundation's funds under any circumstances
  • The Foundation engages in no political activity — our sole mandate is humanitarian
