Our Policy

Aid Disbursement

Financial aid is disbursed at the beginning of each month. All sponsored families continue to receive their monthly support even if a donor is late or has stopped giving. No family is ever left without aid due to a donor's circumstances.

Field Workers

Workers in Egypt are required to visit recipients and offer them love, compassion, and practical help with urgent needs. They submit an annual update for each recipient to ensure aid remains appropriate and targeted.

Remuneration

All members of the Board of Directors are volunteer workers. No director, officer, or member may receive a salary, compensation, or any financial benefit from their position. No officer, director, or member may borrow from the Foundation's funds under any circumstances.

Transparency

The Foundation's books and accounts are verified annually by a reputable Chartered Accountant. Annual tax receipts are issued to all sponsor members.

Scope

The Coptic Aid Foundation acknowledges the difficult situation faced by Christians in Egypt but maintains no political activity. Our sole mandate is humanitarian: helping vulnerable families survive with dignity while sustaining their faith.

We would gladly offer similar services to members of other communities upon request and subject to the availability of funds.

Governance — By-Laws Summary

The following are key excerpts from the By-Laws of the Coptic Aid Foundation:

  • The Foundation is managed by a board of 7 to 12 directors.
  • Each director's term of office is two years.
  • Directors serve without remuneration. No director may directly or indirectly receive profit from their position.
  • No officer, director, or member may borrow from the Foundation's funds under any circumstances.
  • The board may accept legacies, gifts, grants, endowments, and donations of any kind for the purpose of furthering the Foundation's goals.

Accomplishments

Since our founding in 1992, the Coptic Aid Foundation has:

  • Supported over 7,150 needy families across 48 regions of Egypt with monthly aid
  • Funded hundreds of small business projects enabling families to become self-sufficient
  • Supported 728 university students from low-income Coptic families
  • Delivered emergency relief in times of crisis
  • Operated with a 100% volunteer board since inception
  • Earned the blessing and endorsement of Pope Shenouda III, Pope Tawadros II, and over 10 bishops
